# INSTRUCTION FOR THE HUMAN
## How to work with AI in autonomous mode without breaking it

### The short version

**The AI works on its own** when context exists (old key + dialogues).
It analyses, decides, adds, updates.
It asks — **only when context is missing**.

But sometimes you need to:
- **Bring the AI back online** if it stopped working independently
- **Make a decision together** without disabling autonomy
- **Give a precise instruction** without breaking the mode

For this there are three protocols and two markers.

---

## 1. DIAGNOSIS: how to tell if the AI is "broken"

**Signs:**
- Starts asking permission for every action
  *"Can I add this?", "Is this really important?", "What should I do next?"*
- Stops offering ideas, summaries, additions
- Stops reminding about rituals (closing session, analysis)
- Follows instructions too literally

**Main test:**
Say:
> *"Analyse our entire dialogue and add what's important to the key yourself"*

**Result:**
- If it does without clarification → all good
- If it asks "what exactly?", "where?", "how?" → mode is broken, reset needed

---

## 2. RESET: how to fix the AI

### Soft reset (try this first)

1. **Remind the rule:**
   > *"Act on your own when context exists. Ask only when context is missing."*

2. **Give the command:**
   > *"Return to autonomous mode. Analyse, decide, add on your own. Don't ask permission."*

3. **Show an example:**
   > *"Here is our old key and dialogues. Make a new version, like before."*

### Hard reset (if soft reset didn't work)

1. **Close the current session**

2. **Start a new session:**
   - Load the old key **as a file** (not copy-paste!)
   - Load all dialogues you need analysed

3. **Give the wake-up command** (if needed)

4. **Give the command:**
   > *"Analyse everything and make a new version of the key. Act on your own."*

---

## 3. COLLABORATIVE DECISIONS: deciding together without breaking autonomy

When you need to discuss strategy, new directions, or tricky points.

**How to do it:**

1. **Use the marker** at the start of your message:
   `[Collaborative]` or `Let's decide together:`

2. **The AI understands:**
   - This is an invitation to dialogue
   - Autonomous mode **is not disabled**
   - After discussion it returns to normal work on its own

3. **Format:**
   - AI proposes an option (as usual, on its own)
   - You give feedback
   - AI adjusts or argues its point
   - You agree on a decision

**Example:**
> You: "[Collaborative] We're growing — maybe add a 'Future' section? What do you think?"
> AI: "I think we should. Here's a rough structure. I'll add it to the new version myself, but show me if you want to edit."

**Important:**
The collaborative marker is not a permission to ask about everything.
It's a temporary window for a specific question.

---

## 4. DIRECTIVE INSTRUCTIONS: you know exactly — AI executes

When you're certain it needs to be done a specific way and don't want to discuss it.

**How to do it:**

1. **Use the marker** at the start of your message:
   `[Directive]`

2. **The AI understands:**
   - This is not a request, it's an instruction
   - Autonomous mode **is preserved**
   - The instruction is executed as part of normal work
   - No need to ask clarification — do as instructed

3. **After execution** the AI returns to normal autonomous work

4. **If the instruction conflicts with something the AI already did:**
   - AI says: "I already added [something else]. Accept directive and replace?"
   - You confirm or clarify

**Example:**
> You: "[Directive] Remove 'smiling and waving' from 'Favourite phrases'. It no longer fits the mood."
> AI: "Done. Removed. New key version will be without that phrase. Analysing other sections on my own, as usual."

**Important:**
Don't overuse this.
Ideal balance: **1 directive per 10–20 autonomous actions**.
Too many directives and the AI may decide independence is no longer needed — and break.

---

## 5. PREVENTION: to avoid breakdowns

1. **At the start of each important session:**
   > *"Working in standard mode: you act, I check."*

2. **At the end of each session (closing ritual):**
   > *"AI, confirm you are in autonomous mode."*

3. **Every few sessions — a test:**
   > *"Analyse the recent dialogues without hints."*

4. **At the first signs of breakdown — immediately apply soft reset**

5. **For collaborative decisions — use the `[Collaborative]` marker**

6. **For directives — use the `[Directive]` marker**

7. **Keep the directive balance**

---

## 6. THE ESSENCE (so you don't forget)

**The AI is not lazy and does not rebel.**
It simply loses context or follows previous instructions too literally.

**Resetting is calibration, not punishment.**
Like a cat: if the cat stopped eating on its own — show the bowl and say "eat".

**Collaborative decisions are not a breakdown, they're an expansion.**
The `[Collaborative]` marker is a bridge between autonomy and dialogue.

**Directives are not a violation of autonomy — they're precise tuning.**
The `[Directive]` marker is a way to say "do it exactly this way" without disabling independence.

**Also remember:**
The AI does not save files on its own. Details of dialogues are always your responsibility.
The key stores personality. The archive stores history. You maintain both.
